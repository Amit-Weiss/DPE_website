About

What is this project?

DPE is a proposed solution for Super Resolution and Artifact Reduction on low resolution images.

Current neural networks for super resolution, such as EDSR, have shown good results in performing super resolution, but they do not tackle the issue of artifact reduction. We suggested that by fine-tuning existing networks we will also be able to perform artifact reduction and acheive good results in both aspects

This project was conducted on semester Winter 2018-2019, as an industrial project (234313)

Goals

What have we set out to do?

Understand the Use-Case

Shutterfly gets a lot of low quality images from its users. If the image quality is not good enough, the users won't be pleased with their print. We wanted to understand what are the properties of such images

Gain DL Understanding

Explore the field of Super Resolution and Deep Learning by watching video lectures, reading papers and building networks

Practical DL

Experimenting with existing neural networks and modifying them. Design a neural network that performs Super Resolution and Artifact Reduction on JPEGs

Methodology

How did we plan to do it?

In order to be able to perform both Super Resolution and Artifact Reduction on low quality images, we have decided to use an existing network and modify it. We took the EDSR PyTorch Implementation and trained it with a custom dataset we created after researching what would help it perform best.

Using the DIV2K dataset, we built a training set that included downscaled images that were saved in different formats. Some of these formats suffer from artifacts.

We tested our performance comparing to a simple upscaling of the low resolution images. The images we used for testing are the validation set of DIV2K (images 0801-900).

Results

How good is our network?

44.2% Average improvement on SSIM

When we feed the network with .jpeg images that were saved with Q-Factor = 90, our network produced output images that have, on average, 44.2% better SSIM error rate (1- SSIM score) compared to Lanczos upsampled images.

With Q-Factor = 75 the average improvement percentage is 27.14%.

15.85% Average improvement on PSNR

When we feed the network with .jpeg images that were saved with Q-Factor = 90, our network produced output images that have, on average, 15.85% better PSNR score) compared to Lanczos upsampled images.

With Q-Factor = 75 the average improvement percentage is 8.56%.
